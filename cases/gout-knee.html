Case 1 – Gout in the Knee (Easy)

Region: Knee · Pathology: Crystal Arthropathy · Level: Intro

38-year-old male presenting with a 3-week history of left knee pain, swelling, warmth, and reduced range of motion.

Clinical vignette

The patient injured his left knee while running 10 years ago and nearly recovered with physiotherapy. Over the past 5 years, he has experienced intermittent episodes of knee pain and swelling every few months, often triggered by increased alcohol intake or red meat. He has never had classic podagra.

Over the past 3 weeks, he developed an acute flare with warmth, swelling, and limited range of motion, improving partially with celecoxib. He has no inflammatory back pain, dactylitis, uveitis, or constitutional symptoms.

Question 1

Which of the following would you inquire about given the most likely diagnosis?

  • History of Raynaud’s phenomenon
  • History of psoriasis
  • Family history of gout
  • History of preceding GI or GU infection
Explanation

Psoriasis increases gout risk and raises consideration of psoriatic arthritis. Family history supports gout. Preceding GI/GU infection is relevant when considering reactive arthritis. Raynaud’s is less relevant.

Bedside ultrasound

Question 2

What is the ultrasound finding indicated by the yellow arrow?

  • Interface sign (normal artifact)
  • Chondrosis (cartilage loss)
  • Double contour sign
  • Pseudo–double contour sign
Answer

Double contour sign — representing monosodium urate crystal deposition on the surface of articular cartilage.

Question 3

Which investigation is most helpful in confirming the diagnosis?

  • RF and anti-CCP antibodies
  • HLA-B*27
  • Serum urate level
  • MRI of the knee
Explanation

Elevated serum urate supports gout. MRI can show synovitis but is not diagnostic. Arthrocentesis with crystal analysis is confirmatory.

Final diagnosis

Reveal

Oligoarticular gout flare

Supported by dietary triggers, elevated uric acid, ultrasound double contour sign, and negatively birefringent monosodium urate crystals on synovial fluid analysis.
