Case 1 – Gout in the Knee (Easy)

Region: Knee · Pathology: Crystal Arthropathy · Level: Intro

38-year-old male presenting with 3 weeks of left knee pain with warmth, swelling, and limited range of motion (improving with celecoxib).

Clinical vignette

He injured this knee while running 10 years ago and nearly recovered with physiotherapy, but has had intermittent episodes of left knee pain and swelling for ~5 years, sometimes triggered by camping, increased alcohol intake, or red meat. No classic podagra.

He is otherwise well, without inflammatory back pain, dactylitis, uveitis, or constitutional symptoms.
